Slowly, as you die


A whisper, a smile.
I watch you as a lie
On that fateful cot,
Slowly, as you die.
Days, months and years,
They all pass by.
As tears stream down my face,
Slowly, I watch you die.

My mind burns with memories,
Fading and fleeting,
The only thing constant
Is your heart beating.
But I know it'll stop soon,
Each moment I wonder why,
Why you, why me,
Slowly, I watch you die.

These eyes, they weep to no end,
This body, it feels hollow from inside.
Every breath you take,
I'll be by your side.
With tears that could fill oceans,
Everyday I cry.
The pain, the grief, impossible to overcome.
Slowly, I watch you die.

It may take your body,
It may take your heart,
But your soul belongs with mine,
Yet death tries to do us part.
And you lie there, still,
Inhaling, exhaling, with an occasional sigh,
I know you're going away,
Slowly, I watch you die.


I can't do this anymore,
I can't see the light leave your eyes.
Every night I think of it,
My soul cries.
And when I say I won't come back tomorrow,
I know it's a lie,
Because I love you.
Yet, slowly, I watch you die.
