LOST LOVE

The bedsheet looks near new, few creases yet have made way,

Oh but I have always been this messy, a little more may be,

But you see I have learned to neaten them overtime.

I no more sleep on crumpled sheets.

There have been sleepless nights, I agree,

With almost every alternate day, the reason being lurid,

You expected your eyes?

I often reimagine my nightmares the following night,

So that I don’t dream of you again.

Yet I yell every time I dream of you-

On the other side of bed-

Staring at me, smiling, cringing at me snoring, yet,

Smiling.

I would sit up with the dampness soaking my cheeks,

Turning them into a rich shade of red, exactly the way you liked.

So I splatter my face with another round of tears,

Smoldering them, smothering them, my fears.

And neaten up the sheets another time,

To assure my heart you couldn’t be there, again, this time.

To assure my heart you couldn’t be there, again, this time.

You see, I made sure no one found your corpse in that ditch,

Wrapped in the old sheets, you said, kept you warm on nights like this.

Oh but I have always been this messy, a little more may be,

Last night they found you, your face cringing in sleep.

I wonder if they buried you right,

For creaseless sheets disturbed your slumber,

And I prefer you sound asleep.
