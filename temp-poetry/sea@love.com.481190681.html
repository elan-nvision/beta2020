Cycles

Tracing autumn leaves on note books, 

I sit back and see how

My school blew them into classrooms,

And now, my college does that too.

But the leaves are yellower than ever,

And dry and delicate and bitter.

In a different time, in a different city

Not many winters ago,

The leaves were ochre and tuscany,

And smelt of some sweet dampened secret.

Have you ever seen all doors close?

Have you ever been that close?

That you shut your own door and spiral down 

Like an autumn leaf on the ground.

It is strange, 

To only remember how it ended

Now, some mornings begin with Kalidasa,

His poetry- rich and sublime

It is full of faults, and we see

How centuries-old poetry cements convoluted conceptions,

It is the power of verse alone, that can stir even the dead

On some mornings, we read ancient Tamil poetry

Versified by Ilanko, where chaste women  

By default have absent husbands,

And Kannaki's rage like a wildfire grows

The idea that angry women are goddesses

And women otherwise, are chaste.

But predestination, is the idea that grips me-

Do we already know, 

The things about to happen to us?

It is not we but they, the text says

And without knowing the journey,

Are we destined(./?)

Questions are a means without an end

And they flow unaware in a single direction, oblivious

So I ask,

Why can’t our new lives, have old people?

Truth speaks, when you perceived

You saw them in it, but you outgrow sepia-hued pictures

And photo frames fail to stretch across your high-walled boundaries.

The remnants of that Winter, of cruel death

Now lies cold in a picture, alone.

The pain comes alive and ignites my memory,

The spark that was; consumes me in slow flames

I wish, 

I could live my mornings in a better way

Inhale the tang of fresh shiuli and feel it’s cool breath

But my mornings are broken, 

With the trail of faded wild blossoms from the last ever night 

Feeling like no young heart ever should- caught within opposing time zones,

There is no belonging for people like me.

Our brains are caught up in the grey spaces forever,

This generation withdraws from itself.

Help condemned and without hope, we remember the pain

And in that the end,

For our pain is bottomless, baseless, barren

Sprouting nothingness, not even weeds

It sheds, losing itself l

Just like an autumn leaf within us.
