Eternity

Gently, I wash upon the shore,
Gliding, on the soft, warm sand.
Delivering joy — a shell, a smile,
On a child's face.
An endless to and fro,
Kissing the feet of men,
Fishermen, tourists, children— them all,
Clinging along,
The sand on their toes,
The shine in their eyes.

The wind, my friend,
We whisper together,
The secrets of what lies beneath,
These surfaces shimmering blue.
And when night falls,
The calmness— swept away,
And our nocturnal hearts,
Soar up and down,
Roaring from within,
At the sight of the pearly moon.

But come dawn,
A scansion of silence,
And lo! Returns,
The calmness and the peace.
And every night,
More evanescent than the previous,
And every day, brighter.
Gently I wash upon the shore,
An eternity since,
With an eternity still.
