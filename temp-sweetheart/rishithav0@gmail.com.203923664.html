The lights danced, you danced, I tried.

The music turned down a notch and then another until it was just you.

You danced to the music I couldn’t hear.

You were right there.

So close, so far

Blue, purple and pink.

Slurred light and hazy thoughts.

I wish I had the courage. I still don’t.

You looked beautiful that night.

Your head up high, my hands itched to smooth out your curls, they had a mind of their own you see. 

I wanted you to look my way. See me the way you did others. 

So I danced. Danced like there was no tomorrow to the music I couldn’t hear.

To get you to be the one to start the conversation, to have you take the first step.

You were chaos, mischief and everything I shouldn’t want. 

The crowd jumped and moved a tangle of limbs and emotions.

The ground shook. Maybe it would give way and let me fall through.

I would’ve gone with it.

No flailing arms, no struggle. 

And it’s been a while since we were together.

I wish I had the courage to walk across the hall and hold your hand. To lace my fingers in yours. But I didn’t. I don’t.

And then you looked my way. Not through me but at me. And I felt the electricity. The heat. I remember clammy hands and all my firsts. And trust me when I say, I’d want you have all my firsts and my lasts.

So tell me when you’re ready.

I’ll wait till the music comes back to focus.
