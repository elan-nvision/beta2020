Treasured in the heart of the perennial woods, the banquet hall seemed to be gleaming in the radiance of sunshine. As the aroma of the delicacies traversed through the air, the 'boat' which was meant to embark on an enchanting journey in the stream nearby was dropped down while the wet crimson paint was still drooping off its surface and the kids made made a dash for the kitchen. Had the room not only been brightly illuminated thanks to the scintillating chandeliers' , her flamboyance seemed to lit up the crowd as she cautiously stepped down the stairs, gorgeously dressed up in a purple frock aided with a platinum ring and diamond studded earrings. Her father looked up to her and smiled, silently crying inside for in a week she would be heading off to New York with her husband. A good man, he thought. Well educated, a prestigious job, benevolent, responsible.

He anxiously swiped up his phone, as he received a call from an anonymous number.

“ Am I speaking to Mr. Gupta?” the voice said.

“ Yes, may I know who’s this?”

“ We are very sorry for your loss… we found Mr. Rajesh by the jogging track this morning with multiple stab wounds…...."

His phone slipped from his hand as the clouds of darkness and silence seemed to cast their spell over. The brilliant red wine embraced the ground and the glass shattered into a million little pieces. For a moment he didn’t know how to react. The lights seemed to fade out, and there she sat in the corner all by herself.

“ Happy birthday to me…..” she sang in a distressed voice as she devoured a giant chunk of the majestic red velvet cake and relished the blood of her fiancé.
