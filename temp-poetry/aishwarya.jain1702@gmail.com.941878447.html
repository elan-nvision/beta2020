Roses

There's a garden, I hear, of roses nearby.
It's beautiful, they all say,
For that's where all the lovers' hearts lie.
The roses, their scent mixes with the love in the air,
And that's where the young men go,
They say, to find maidens pretty and fair.
Each a different colour, each with a story of its own,
I hear it's an abode of beauty,
Fullfilled by lush of De Groen*.
But can't they see the truth laid out?
The garden of roses that they see,
Is the garden of roses I doubt.
And my opinion, it is often debated.
But what I see is what I see,
A garden of roses, is just a garden of thorns, decorated.

*De Groen is Dutch for "The Green"
