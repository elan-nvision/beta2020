Two little creatures sat down under a summer sky,

Wrapped up in their tiny, inconsequential lives,

Their hushed voices punctuated by smiles and silences.

And under that hazy blue sky, they dreamed a world

A world so fair and so good,

That it might remain a dream forever,

And never become anything more

But nevertheless, what a world it was

Where the children laughed, people loved

Where hate was just a forgotten word

Countries, they seemed to get along

So well that you'd wonder if they even existed

Religion, it ceased to be a about a group of people

Instead, it was a path to God

Gender was immaterial- love triumphed over all

So perfect,

These humans had to wonder what bombs were for

Money, they decided, should be done away with

For money leads to power, and power to dictatorship

The universe was a pit of secrets and mysteries

And life was a journey to figure them out

And thus, the little creatures dreamed on and on

As they watched the sun go down

It hid behind a orange layer of angry polluted air for a while

And then disappeared below the earth

The showtime was up, the dream broken up by reality

These two little creatures, they heaved a sigh

One got up, and helped the other to its feet

And stood there, staring at the darkening street

For a moment, just for a moment,

They wondered if they could bring the world in their heads alive,

Wondered what they could do to make it a little better

The light, it left them by then,

And leaving them staring at nothing but darkness

One last smile thrown at each other, that was it

They walked back home,

Back to a world of unequals
