In the arms of oblivion

Standing in the delicate shimmer 

Of the moon, peeking through the clouds

Her hands around a wilting bouquet

Roses waiting to embrace the ground

Neath boughs old and graven

Cold stone slab erect

A symbol of bereavement

The kiss of time left derelict

There hidden within a wooden casket

Years of joy, stolen away

Promises left harrowingly hollow

In their repose they stay

Tears grace her pale cheeks

As she stand precariously at the edge

Of a sea of melancholy, beckoning her gently

To fall deep into it's frigid pledge

And again the night reaches

To take her hand

Guide her to her beloved

Past the shifting sands

Be rid of these seething embers,

That crawl towards her heart

Unwrap these drapes of solitude,

From it's cold caress, depart

And breaking the dusk's silence

The winds weep their dirge

For the tragedy lying upon that dirt

Maybe in heaven their souls will merge
